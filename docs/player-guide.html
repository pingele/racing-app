Fantasy Racing

Player Guide
Predict the finish · Score the points · Top the standings

Fantasy Racing lets you predict the finishing order of every class in a race. When the results come in, you earn points for each driver you placed in exactly the right spot. Points add up across the season on the Standings leaderboard. Here's how to play.

1 Create your account

  1. On the Sign up screen, enter a display name (this is how you'll appear on the leaderboard) and your email.
  2. Choose a password with at least 8 characters, including an uppercase and a lowercase letter, a number, and a symbol. The screen shows a live checklist that ticks off each rule as you type.
  3. You're signed in automatically — no email confirmation needed.
  4. Returning? Use Log in with your email and password.

On a phone, tap the ☰ menu (top-left) to reach Races, Standings, and Scoring.

2 Find a race

Open the Races page. It's organized top to bottom:

  • To predict — open races waiting for your picks.
  • Scored — races with results in Results in
  • All races — a full list you can browse any time.

Tap any race card to open it and see its classes (heats, features, etc.).

3 Make your predictions

  1. Each class shows its full list of entries.
  2. Drag drivers into your predicted finishing order — or use the ▲ / ▼ arrows. Your #1 is your predicted winner.
  3. Press Save prediction. Come back and Update it anytime until the class locks.

🔒 Once an admin locks a class (or the race), predictions are final and can't be changed.

4 Read the standings

  • The Standings page ranks every player by total points across all events.
  • Click a racer to expand their per-event scores.
  • The live Top 5 leaderboard (left side on web, top on mobile) refreshes automatically after each race is scored.

P How scoring works

Scoring is F1-style and position-exact: you earn a spot's points only when the driver you placed there actually finished there. Nail P1 and you bank the top score; get P2 right too and you add those points on top. Being one spot off scores nothing — the match must be exact.

Your race score is the sum across every class in that event; the Standings add up every race for the season. On a scored class you'll see your picks beside the finish — columns Pos · # · Driver · Start · Your pick · Pts — with the rows you nailed highlighted and a You scored N pts badge.
